The Issue: A Flood of Corruption?

An analysis of the 2024 Flood Control Scandal

The Budget vs. The Reality

Under the current administration, the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) was allocated a staggering ₱545 billion for flood control projects. Despite this massive funding—larger than the budget for agriculture or defense—Metro Manila and nearby provinces were submerged during Typhoon Carina in 2024.

  • 421+ "Ghost Projects": Senate hearings revealed hundreds of projects that were paid for but either never existed or were abandoned.
  • Zero Allocations in Critical Areas: Investigations found that flood-prone towns in Bulacan received zero flood control infrastructure, while funds were diverted to non-critical districts favored by politicians.

Allegations of Systemic Corruption

The core of the scandal involves allegations of a "kickback" system embedded in the infrastructure sector.

  • The 20-40% Kickback Scheme: Witnesses at Senate hearings testified that up to 40% of a project's total cost is siphoned off as bribes to legislators and district engineers.
  • Substandard Materials: To recover the cost of bribes, contractors allegedly use cheaper, substandard cement and steel, resulting in dikes and spillways that crumble under pressure.
  • Monopoly of Contracts: A specific group of contractors reportedly cornered billions in contracts.

The Human Cost

The failure is not just financial; it is humanitarian. The lack of effective flood control has led to:

Displacement

Thousands of families in Metro Manila and Bulacan lose their homes annually due to rising waters.

Economic Loss

Billions in agricultural crops and business revenue are wiped out, keeping the poorest sectors in poverty.

Recommendations & Solutions

We propose a multi-faceted approach to fix the flood control crisis, moving from "patchwork politics" to science-based infrastructure.

National Flood Control Transparency Portal

The biggest enabler of the current scandal is the lack of visibility on project progress.

The Fix

Prevent "ghost projects" by mandating a centralized digital portal where every project must have monthly geo-tagged photo updates proving construction progress.

River Basin-Based Budgeting

Budgets are currently "sliced" into small district contracts, leading to uncoordinated, patchwork dikes.

The Fix

Funding should follow the 18 Major River Basins Master Plan. Projects must be integrated across provinces rather than limited by political district boundaries.

Fast-Track High-Impact Projects

Major game-changers like the Parañaque Spillway have been delayed while billions are wasted on small dredging.

The Fix

Prioritize securing ODA (e.g., JICA) for the Parañaque Spillway to drain Laguna de Bay directly to Manila Bay, bypassing the clogged Pasig River.

Citizen Participatory Audits

The Commission on Audit (COA) is understaffed and cannot physically inspect every dike in remote areas.

The Fix

Accredit CSOs and local NGOs as "deputy auditors." Locals must sign a "Certificate of Reality" before final payments are released to contractors.

"Sponge City" Ordinances

Metro Manila is a concrete jungle that prevents rainwater from seeping into the ground.

The Fix

Mandate Nature-Based Solutions (NbS): rainwater cisterns for subdivisions, permeable pavement for parking lots, and wetland restoration.

Decouple Design & Construction

Contractors often influence designs to make them cheaper to build, leading to "politicized engineering."

The Fix

The entity that designs the master plan should not be the one building it. Independent firms must design and supervise to ensure blueprint compliance.
